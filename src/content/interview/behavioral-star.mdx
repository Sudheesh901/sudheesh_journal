## The Better STAR Format

For technical roles, plain STAR is often too shallow. Use:

```text
Situation -> Constraint -> Decision -> Action -> Result -> Reflection
```

The constraint and decision are what make the story senior.

## Story Bank

Maintain stories for:

- Debugging a production issue.
- Reducing model or LLM cost.
- Improving retrieval or recommendation quality.
- Handling ambiguity.
- Disagreeing constructively.
- Shipping under constraints.
- Learning a new domain quickly.

## How to Make It Credible

Use specific numbers where possible, but explain the mechanism behind the impact. For example, cost reduction is stronger when paired with the technical levers: model routing, prompt compression, caching, batching, or retrieval improvement.

## Common Mistakes

- Telling the story as a list of tasks.
- Hiding the tradeoff.
- Not explaining your personal contribution.
- Ending at the result without reflection.
- Using too much internal company context.

## Preparation Habit

Write each story in 8-10 lines. Then compress it to a 90-second version. The short version forces clarity.
