## The Deep Learning Lens

Deep learning is representation learning. Instead of manually defining every feature, we define architectures and objectives that can learn useful internal representations from data.

The central questions are:

- What structure exists in the input?
- What architecture can exploit that structure?
- What objective teaches the representation we need?
- What scale of data and compute is required?
- What deployment limits shape the final model?

## Architecture Map

### Feedforward Networks

Useful for dense features and as building blocks inside larger systems.

### CNNs

Designed for local spatial structure. Still useful for image, audio, and efficient feature extraction.

### RNNs and Sequence Models

Historically important for sequential data. They teach useful ideas about state, recurrence, and temporal dependence.

### Transformers

Transformers use attention to let tokens condition on other tokens directly. They scale well, parallelize well, and are the foundation of modern LLMs.

## Optimization Notes

Training is controlled by loss landscapes, gradients, initialization, normalization, learning rates, batch size, and regularization. A model failure may be an optimization failure, not an architecture failure.

## Researcher Checklist

Before changing architecture, check:

1. Is the dataset clean and representative?
2. Is the baseline implemented correctly?
3. Is the training loop stable?
4. Are train and validation curves interpretable?
5. Are errors clustered by data slice?
6. Is the model too large for the available data?

## Production Constraints

Deep learning models are not only evaluated by quality. Production adds:

- Latency
- Memory
- Throughput
- Batchability
- Cost per request
- Observability
- Rollback strategy

## Failure Modes

- Overfitting hidden by a weak validation split.
- Silent distribution shift after deployment.
- Poor calibration even with high accuracy.
- Training instability from learning-rate or normalization issues.
- Serving bottlenecks caused by model size rather than API code.
